Introduction

Welcome to Jarie Suisen!!! YAY! My chance to shine and share the things I like! My weird logo says it well: Here, I want to share the things I enjoy, like the pouring of information from an open faucet. (I hope the attempt at a punny title actually makes sense to Japanese readers. And, yes, I know that おすすめ would be the typical choice to state that something is a suggestion. I have my reason for it.) I guess you could consider this like a "top 10 list"... but as a full site, and without the stupid ranking. Sure, I might compare two series against each other (not that I like to do so). But I definitely don't waste time ranking things overall. That's just sad.
Now, why are you here? I dunno, you opened the page. I hope it's because you want to find a new book, show, podcast, game, etc. If not, hey, why not see what things you didn't know about before?

Rules

Pfft, rules? Yeah, right. If you're here, I just hope you find something new to enjoy! However, I do have a few standards I follow in how I list and organize things.
1) Original-language names first. If the original title or author's name is from a non-English language, then it will be posted first, with the English version of the title or name in parenthesis afterwards. I quite dislike that the layout on sites like Wikipedia make it feel like the English title is the original and that there is a "Japanese version" of it. NO! The Japanese title is the original! (Unless it isn't, in which case I won't list that.) "Why are they even making a different title for the English version!? It's not even a translation, they went and added stuff that don't help the title make sense!" And frustrated, ranty thoughts like that.
2) I don't really put things in any particular order. I'll try to keep things alphabetical, but that's not always the best choice. Either way, I'll try to keep it consistent for similar types of lists.
3) I'm new to this and not particularly interested in design, so the site may feel a bit drab. I hope to improve it over time, but the main point is a place to document and share things.
4) Some of my opinions may be more rants than anything else. Sorry if this happens! (I've just started, so who knows what will happen.)
